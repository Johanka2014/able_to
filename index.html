When to Use "Can", "Could", and "Be Able To"

1. Can

"Can" is the most common modal verb. We use it to talk about ability, permission, and possibility in the present.

Usage Example Czech Translation (Překlad)
Ability (schopnost) I can speak English. Umím mluvit anglicky.
Permission (povolení) Can I go to the party? Můžu jít na večírek?
Possibility (možnost) It can get cold here at night. V noci se zde může ochladit.

2. Could

"Could" is used for past ability, polite requests, and suggestions.

Usage Example Czech Translation (Překlad)
Past Ability (minulá schopnost) When I was young, I could run very fast. Když jsem byl mladý, uměl jsem běhat velmi rychle.
Polite Request (zdvořilá žádost) Could you help me, please? Mohl byste mi, prosím, pomoci?
Suggestion (návrh) We could go to the cinema tonight. Mohli bychom jít dnes večer do kina.

3. Be Able To

"Be able to" is more formal and can be used in all tenses. It is necessary when we want to use other tenses like future or present perfect.

Usage Example Czech Translation (Překlad)
Future (budoucnost) Soon I will be able to drive a car. Brzy budu moci řídit auto.
Present Perfect (předpřítomný čas) I haven't been able to finish my homework. Nedokázal jsem dokončit svůj domácí úkol.
Specific Occasion in the Past He was able to escape the fire. (Not 'could') Podařilo se mu uniknout z požáru.
